The Full Story

Seven Decades of Going Anywhere

From a wartime jeep copy to the world's most trusted 4×4 — the Land Cruiser's history is the story of Toyota itself: relentless, pragmatic, unstoppable.

Origin
1951
BJ — The Toyota Jeep
Born out of a Japanese military contract, the BJ was Toyota's answer to the Willys Jeep. It impressed everyone — including General Douglas MacArthur's forces — when it climbed Mt. Fuji's Fujinomiya trail in 1951. The nameplate "Land Cruiser" was officially adopted in 1954, chosen to rival Land Rover.
BJFJMilitaryJapan Postwar
20 Series
1955
The First True Series
The 20-series (FJ25) brought a more refined body, better engine options, and marked Toyota's first serious export push. Australia, the Middle East, and Africa began to take notice. Rugged and simple, it could be maintained with basic tools anywhere on earth.
FJ25ExportAustralia
40 Series
1960
The Icon. The One Everyone Knows.
The FJ40 is arguably the most beloved Land Cruiser ever made. Produced for 24 years with minimal changes — because why fix perfection? Boxy, tough, simple. It became the vehicle of choice for UN relief missions, Australian cattle stations, African safari operators, and weekend warriors alike. Its straight-six engine could run on low-octane fuel, cook oil, or sheer stubbornness.
FJ4024 YearsIconGlobal
55 Series
1967
The Station Wagon Arrives
Toyota introduced a longer wheelbase, wagon-bodied variant for families who refused to compromise on capability. The 55-series could carry eight passengers across terrain that would stop a regular car dead. This was the genesis of the Land Cruiser's dual identity: family hauler by day, expedition rig by weekend.
FJ55Station WagonFamily
60 Series
1980
Comfort Enters the Equation
The 60-series softened the formula — deliberately. Power steering, optional air conditioning, proper interior trim. Critics worried Toyota was going soft. They were wrong. The 60-series outsold everything before it, proving there was a market for capable comfort. The FJ62 even got fuel injection. Radical.
FJ60FJ62Power SteeringAC
70 Series
1984
Still in Production. No, Really.
The 70-series launched in 1984 and — in various markets — is still being built today. That's not a typo. For 40 years it has served militaries, aid organisations, miners, and farmers who need a vehicle that simply will not die. No touchscreen. No air suspension. Just coil springs, a diesel engine, and the promise that it'll start tomorrow morning.

80 Series
1990
The Pinnacle. Full Stop.
Many enthusiasts consider the FJ80 the greatest Land Cruiser ever made. Coil springs all around, a proper locking centre differential, optional front and rear lockers, and enough interior comfort to use as a daily driver. The 1FZ-FE inline-six was virtually indestructible. These trucks hold their value better than gold. Finding a clean one today will cost you.
FJ80HDJ80Coil SpringsLockersLegend
100 Series
1998
The Luxury Turn
The 100-series went full luxury. Independent front suspension divided the fanbase — purists mourned, buyers celebrated. It competed directly with Range Rover and Mercedes G-Wagen at the premium end. Available with a twin-turbo diesel that made serious power, the 100 proved you could have a leather-lined cabin and still cross the Sahara.
HDJ100UZJ100IFSLuxury
200 Series
2007
The Long Reign
The 200-series ruled for 14 years — one of the longest production runs of the modern era. It gained a Kinetic Dynamic Suspension System, Crawl Control, and Multi-Terrain Select. Technology was catching up to capability. Still not a thing you'd choose to break down. Almost nothing could go wrong, and even if it did — you could usually fix it.

300 Series
2021
Into the Future — Without Losing Its Soul
The 300-series dropped the V8 for a twin-turbo V6, shedding 200kg in the process. Buyers were sceptical. Then they drove it. More power, better economy, sharper dynamics. A new TNGA-F platform. Multi-terrain cameras that show you what the front wheels are doing. A 70th-anniversary edition in 2021 for the faithful. The legend, updated — not replaced.

Why It Matters

A Legacy Built on Proof

🌍

Every Continent

Land Cruisers have crossed every continent on Earth, including Antarctica. No other production vehicle has a comparable record of genuine global deployment.

🏥

Humanitarian Workhorse

The Land Cruiser is the vehicle of choice for the UN, Red Cross, Médecins Sans Frontières, and hundreds of NGOs. When access matters most, there's one call to make.

🗺️

The Overland Standard

Ask any serious overlander what they trust their life to across 10,000 miles of remote terrain. The answer is almost always the same. Parts exist everywhere. Knowledge is shared freely.

📈

Holds Its Value

A well-maintained FJ80 or 100-series appreciates rather than depreciates. The Land Cruiser community has turned these trucks into cult objects — and the market agrees.

🔧

Fix It Anywhere

Toyota built the Land Cruiser to be repairable by a mechanic in a village with hand tools and locally-sourced parts. That philosophy has never left the DNA.

🤝

A Real Community

Land Cruiser owners wave at each other on trails. They share recovery gear, knowledge, and campfires. Clubs exist on every continent. This one's for the adults who still get excited.
